The USD/CAD pair softened slightly on Friday, retreating from a one-week high as traders digested recent gains. During the Asian session, the currency pair traded with a mild negative bias but held firm above the mid-1.3600s, signaling underlying support. Investors are cautious ahead of key employment reports from both the United States and Canada, which could steer the next major move for the pair.  

Market participants are closely watching the data for clues on labor market strength, which may influence monetary policy expectations in both countries. While short-term momentum has eased, the pair’s proximity to last week’s highs suggests lingering bullish sentiment, with 1.3650 remaining a near-term reference point ahead of the jobs releases.
