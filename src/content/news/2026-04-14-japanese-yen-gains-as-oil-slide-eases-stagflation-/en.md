The Japanese Yen (JPY) strengthened against the US Dollar (USD) as falling oil prices eased fears of stagflation in Japan. Investors reacted positively to the decline in energy costs, which reduced pressure on inflation and economic growth, supporting the currency’s appeal. Market participants noted that this relief came after weeks of heightened oil volatility, which had previously weighed on consumer sentiment and corporate margins in the country.  

Despite the yen’s gains, lingering geopolitical tensions in the Middle East kept energy markets jittery, sustaining expectations for a possible near-term rate hike by the Bank of Japan (BoJ). Analysts highlighted that any shift in oil prices could quickly influence BoJ policy outlook, keeping traders attentive to both energy developments and central bank signals.
