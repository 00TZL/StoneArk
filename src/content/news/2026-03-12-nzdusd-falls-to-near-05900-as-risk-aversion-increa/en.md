NZD/USD extended its losses for the third consecutive day, dropping to around 0.5900 during Thursday's Asian session. The pair is under pressure as global risk aversion intensifies due to the ongoing conflict in the Middle East, which has spooked market sentiment.

The US Dollar (USD) continues to outperform, supported by rising energy prices, which are fueling concerns about inflation and diminishing expectations for Federal Reserve interest rate cuts. This combination of geopolitical uncertainty and persistent inflation risks has contributed to the NZD/USD’s continued weakness.
