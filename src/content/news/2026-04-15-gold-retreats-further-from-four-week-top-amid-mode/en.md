Gold (XAU/USD) eased further from a nearly four-week peak reached during Wednesday’s Asian session, slipping toward a fresh daily low near $1,815 in the last hour. The retreat comes as the US dollar shows modest signs of recovery, weighing on bullion’s recent momentum. Despite the pullback, gains over the past sessions have kept gold well-supported, limiting the scope for a deeper slide.  

Market watchers note that while the dollar’s mild rebound pressures gold, downside appears contained as broader risk sentiment remains cautious. Traders are closely monitoring technical levels and economic cues, with $1,810-$1,815 acting as near-term support, suggesting any further declines could be shallow before a potential rebound.
