Indian Rupee (INR) advanced in early trade on Friday, supported by easing global crude oil prices, recovering after registering modest losses against the US Dollar (USD) on Thursday.  

Market sentiment remained sensitive to oil price movements, with India’s import-heavy energy profile providing support to the rupee as crude benchmarks softened across Asian trading hours. Traders also monitored US dollar demand trends, as expectations of stable Federal Reserve policy kept the greenback range-bound against major currencies. Analysts noted that sustained weakness in crude oil could further cushion INR losses, although near-term volatility may persist due to global risk sentiment and capital flow adjustments in emerging Asian markets. Overall bias remains mildly supportive for INR in short-term trading sessions ahead of opening.
