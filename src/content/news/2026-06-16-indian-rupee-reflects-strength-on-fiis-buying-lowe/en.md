Indian Rupee (INR) extended gains against the US Dollar (USD) on Tuesday, reflecting renewed strength in domestic currency markets. The move came as sustained foreign institutional investor (FII) buying supported risk appetite, while easing crude oil prices reduced India’s import bill pressure. Traders noted improved sentiment across emerging market currencies amid softer global dollar demand.

Market participants highlighted that sustained FII inflows combined with lower Brent crude prices continue to underpin INR stability, limiting downside pressure against the USD. However, analysts cautioned that volatility in US yields and upcoming macroeconomic data could cap further gains. Near-term trading is expected to remain range-bound, with import demand and RBI interventions also closely watched by currency traders in coming trading sessions ahead overall.
