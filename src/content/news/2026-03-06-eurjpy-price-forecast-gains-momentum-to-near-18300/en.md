The EUR/JPY pair edged higher on Friday, trading around 182.95 in early European hours as bullish momentum continues. The cross remains above its 100-day exponential moving average (EMA), signaling that the ongoing uptrend could extend toward the 183.00 mark. Traders appear confident in the Euro, supported by steady demand amid global risk appetite.  

Market focus remains on the Bank of Japan (BoJ) and its uncertain interest rate trajectory, which may keep the Japanese Yen under pressure. Any hints of policy shifts from the BoJ could trigger volatility, but for now, the Euro maintains strength against the Yen. Analysts suggest that as long as the 100-day EMA holds, EUR/JPY could test fresh highs in the near term.
