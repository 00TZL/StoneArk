The Indian Rupee faced continued pressure on Tuesday as a risk-off sentiment bolstered the US Dollar. USD/INR extended gains for a third consecutive session, hovering around 95.40 during Asian trading hours. Market participants cited global uncertainty and cautious investor sentiment as key drivers behind the rupee’s subdued performance, keeping the pair in focus ahead of critical economic indicators.  

Traders are now turning attention to India’s upcoming economic data, with the HSBC Composite and Services Purchasing Managers’ Index (PMI) scheduled for release on Wednesday. Analysts suggest the figures could influence short-term currency movements, offering clues on domestic growth momentum and service sector activity. USD/INR dynamics are expected to remain sensitive to both global risk trends and domestic data releases.
