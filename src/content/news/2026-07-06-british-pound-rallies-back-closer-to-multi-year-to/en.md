British pound extends its recovery against the Japanese yen, with GBP/JPY gaining strong follow-through momentum for a second successive day. The cross climbs into the mid-216.00s during the early European session on Monday, edging back toward the multi-year peak reached in late April. Support comes as carry trade demand strengthens while fiscal concerns continue to weigh on the yen.

Market participants note that the yen remains pressured by expectations of sustained loose monetary policy in Japan, keeping carry trade flows active. Meanwhile, sterling benefits from relatively firmer risk sentiment and steady positioning ahead of key macro releases. Traders will watch whether GBP/JPY can sustain gains above the mid-216 handle or face profit-taking near recent highs in coming trading sessions ahead.
