The AUD/JPY pair slipped to around 113.50 in early European trade on Monday, shedding some of its recent gains. Investors are reacting cautiously amid escalating tensions between the United States and Iran following the seizure of a vessel in the Gulf, prompting a shift toward safer assets. The Australian Dollar (AUD) faces pressure as market participants reassess risk sentiment, while the Japanese Yen (JPY) benefits from its safe-haven appeal.  

Traders noted that the pair’s retreat near 113.50 reflects a broader trend of risk aversion in the forex market. Analysts suggest that if geopolitical tensions continue, the Yen could strengthen further against the AUD. Market watchers remain focused on updates from the US and Iran for potential catalysts that could drive additional volatility.
