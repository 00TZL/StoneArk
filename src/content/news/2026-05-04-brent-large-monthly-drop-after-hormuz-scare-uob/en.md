Brent crude saw its largest monthly percentage drop since December 2025, according to UOB strategists. The commodity experienced a significant decline, despite a brief surge above USD 126 per barrel due to renewed concerns over the Strait of Hormuz.

The scare, which led to fears of disruptions in global oil supply, initially pushed Brent prices higher. However, this spike proved short-lived, and the market ultimately closed the month in the red. The sizeable drop reflects broader market dynamics and investor sentiment, overshadowing the temporary spike driven by geopolitical tensions.
