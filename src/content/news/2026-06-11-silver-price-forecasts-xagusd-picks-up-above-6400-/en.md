Silver prices rebounded on Thursday, with XAG/USD climbing above the $64.00 mark and reaching an intraday high of $64.50 at the time of writing. The recovery followed a sharp decline earlier in the session, when the white metal fell to a two-and-a-half-month low of $61.50. Improved market sentiment helped support prices after reports indicated that negotiations between the United States and Iran were continuing.

Despite the bounce, the broader technical outlook remains bearish, suggesting sellers still retain control of the market. While easing geopolitical concerns provided short-term relief and encouraged bargain hunting, silver remains under pressure after recent losses. Traders are closely monitoring developments surrounding the US-Iran talks, as any further progress could influence risk sentiment and drive additional volatility in precious metals markets.
