West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude extended its upward trajectory for a second consecutive session, trading around $95.80 during Asian hours on Tuesday. Investors are keeping a close eye on supply disruptions, as tensions in the Middle East are raising concerns over the stability of critical oil transit routes. The ongoing geopolitical friction has reinforced a bullish sentiment in energy markets, driving WTI prices closer to the $96.00 mark.

Market watchers note that these supply worries are weighing on global crude availability, supporting prices despite mixed economic signals elsewhere. Traders remain sensitive to developments in the region, with any escalation likely to prompt further volatility. Analysts suggest that the current price action reflects both immediate risk premiums and underlying tightness in global oil inventories, underscoring the market’s vulnerability to geopolitical shocks.
