US Dollar Index (DXY) traded near 101.20 in early European trading hours on Thursday, slipping below the 101.50 level as market participants adjusted positions ahead of key US data releases. The index, which tracks the greenback against a basket of six major currencies, showed mild downside pressure but remained broadly supported within its recent range.

Despite the short-term pullback, the broader outlook for the US dollar remains cautiously bullish as traders focus on upcoming Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) data. Expectations of resilient labor market conditions continue to underpin demand for the currency, while volatility is likely to increase as investors reposition ahead of the release. Any surprise in job growth or wages could trigger sharp directional moves in the index upward.
