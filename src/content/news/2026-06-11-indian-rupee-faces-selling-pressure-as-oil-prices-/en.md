The Indian Rupee (INR) opened lower against the US Dollar (USD) on Thursday, with the USD/INR pair climbing toward 95.75. The local currency is under pressure as oil prices rebound sharply, driven by concerns over the potential breakdown of the ceasefire between the United States and Iran. Traders are weighing the impact of rising crude costs on India’s import bill, which has contributed to the selling momentum in the rupee.

Market participants note that the currency weakness reflects broader risk sentiment, as heightened geopolitical tensions tend to favor the safe-haven dollar. The INR’s decline at the start of trading underscores investor caution, with the USD/INR pair testing fresh short-term highs amid ongoing uncertainty in energy markets and international diplomacy.
