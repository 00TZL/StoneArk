Japanese yen underperforms against major currencies during Monday’s European session, extending recent softness as traders reassess Bank of Japan policy outlook. The USD/JPY pair trades marginally lower around 160.15, recovering earlier session losses after brief downside pressure in Asia.

Market sentiment remains cautious as uncertainty over the BoJ’s rate path continues to weigh on the yen, limiting upside attempts despite intermittent risk-off flows. USD strength and widening yield differentials also provide underlying support for the pair, keeping the 160 handle in focus for traders watching intervention risk from Japanese authorities. Overall, traders remain attentive to upcoming macro signals from Japan and the US that could influence near-term volatility and direction in the currency pair as liquidity conditions remain relatively thin.
