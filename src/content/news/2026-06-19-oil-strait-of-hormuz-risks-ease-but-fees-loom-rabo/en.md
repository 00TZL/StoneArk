Oil markets saw geopolitical risk premium ease after Rabobank noted that a 60-day US–Iran memorandum of understanding has reduced immediate threats to the global economy by keeping the Strait of Hormuz open. However, analysts stress that shipping traffic through the chokepoint remains constrained, limiting full normalization of flows.

Despite improved diplomatic tone, market participants caution that commercial passage is still restricted, with additional fees and insurance costs expected to persist for tankers using the route. Traders note that any disruption risk is now lower but not removed, and freight pricing may remain volatile. The situation continues to influence energy-linked forex flows and regional risk sentiment while keeping attention on supply chain bottlenecks and potential policy shifts ahead of OPEC decisions.
