The New Zealand Dollar weakened against the US Dollar on Tuesday, with NZD/USD slipping back below the mid-0.5800s during the European session as investors turned cautious amid rising geopolitical tensions linked to Iran. The risk-sensitive Kiwi came under renewed pressure as demand for the safe-haven Greenback strengthened across currency markets.

Market participants also remained sidelined ahead of the Reserve Bank of New Zealand’s policy decision, limiting support for the NZD. Traders are closely watching the RBNZ for guidance on the interest-rate outlook and any signals on the domestic economic backdrop. The combination of risk aversion and central bank uncertainty kept the NZD/USD pair on the defensive through the session.
