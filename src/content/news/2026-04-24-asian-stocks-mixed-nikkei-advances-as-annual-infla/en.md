Asian forex markets were mixed on Friday as currency flows tracked uneven regional equity performance and shifting risk sentiment. The Nikkei advanced, supported by expectations that annual inflation remains below the Bank of Japan’s 2% target, reinforcing a cautious monetary outlook ahead of policy signals. The yen held relatively steady as traders balanced softer domestic inflation pressures with broader dollar demand across Asia.

Elsewhere, Asian equities mostly declined as higher oil prices pressured regional currencies and risk appetite. Markets reacted to stalled US–Iran peace talks and ongoing disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz, which kept energy-linked forex volatility elevated. Safe-haven flows supported the dollar, while commodity currencies softened amid concerns over tightening global supply conditions and inflation spillovers across major Asian trading sessions and currency trading desks overall.
