The Japanese Yen (JPY) has surrendered earlier gains against the US Dollar (USD) on Friday, with the USD/JPY pair climbing back above the 160.00 level. This threshold is widely viewed as the maximum level of yen weakness tolerated by Japanese authorities, who remain vigilant to curb excessive currency depreciation. Market participants are closely monitoring the situation, as volatility persists amid a lack of fresh direction for the yen.  

Investors are also awaiting clearer signals on the ongoing conflict in Iran, which continues to influence risk sentiment and safe-haven flows. The uncertainty surrounding geopolitical developments has limited the yen’s ability to sustain strength, prompting traders to recalibrate positions and contributing to the USD/JPY rebound above the critical 160 mark.
