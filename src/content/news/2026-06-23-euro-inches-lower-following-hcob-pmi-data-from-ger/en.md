The euro edged slightly lower on Tuesday after fresh HCOB Purchasing Managers’ Index data from Germany weighed on sentiment. EUR/USD remained broadly steady, following modest losses in the previous session, and traded near 1.1430 during European trading hours. Market participants monitored eurozone activity signals, as the German PMI print pointed to softer momentum in the region’s manufacturing and services sectors.

However, the pair extended its retreat after losing its earlier intraday gains, as traders reacted to the latest German HCOB PMI release. The data reinforced concerns over uneven eurozone growth, prompting mild profit-taking in EUR/USD. Despite the pullback, the pair maintained support around 1.1430, with investors awaiting further macroeconomic cues and upcoming central bank commentary for direction this week ahead.
