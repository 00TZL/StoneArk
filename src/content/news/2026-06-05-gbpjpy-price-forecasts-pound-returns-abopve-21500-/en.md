The GBP/JPY pair has rebounded above the 215.00 level, benefiting from broad-based weakness in the Japanese Yen. Traders noted that the Pound found support after the Yen’s modest recovery attempt during Friday’s Asian session quickly faded, leaving the cross to regain upward momentum. Market sentiment remains tilted toward risk-on positions, as global investors favor higher-yielding currencies over the traditionally defensive Yen. Technical indicators suggest the pair could continue testing resistance levels around 216.00 if bullish momentum persists.

Meanwhile, the Yen’s brief strength appears limited, as safe-haven flows remain subdued. Analysts caution that any further Yen recovery may be shallow unless global risk sentiment shifts sharply. For now, GBP/JPY traders are closely watching macroeconomic cues from both the UK and Japan, as well as broader market risk appetite, to gauge the next directional move. The cross’s current trajectory signals a continuation of the recent upward trend unless significant intervention or unexpected data emerges.
