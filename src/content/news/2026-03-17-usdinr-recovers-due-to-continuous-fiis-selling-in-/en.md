The Indian Rupee (INR) opened lower against the US Dollar (USD) on Tuesday, following a decline on Monday. The USD/INR pair saw a recovery to around 92.90 as the Indian Rupee resumed its downward trajectory, pressured by persistent outflows from the Indian stock market.

The continuous selling by Foreign Institutional Investors (FIIs) in Indian equities continues to weigh on the INR. With foreign funds exiting, market sentiment remains bearish, further driving the INR lower against the US Dollar. As a result, the USD/INR pair shows signs of stabilizing near the 92.90 level, indicating that the Rupee's downside pressure may persist in the near term.
