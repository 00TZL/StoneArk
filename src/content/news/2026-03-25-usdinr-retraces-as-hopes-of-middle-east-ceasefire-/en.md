The Indian Rupee (INR) opened modestly stronger against the US Dollar (USD) on Wednesday, as market sentiment improved following growing hopes for a ceasefire in the Middle East. The USD/INR pair retraced to around 94.30, retreating from its previous day’s record high of 94.75. 

The pullback came as investors reacted positively to the potential for stability in the region, easing concerns over global risk. Despite the decline, the USD/INR remains elevated, with the currency pair reflecting ongoing pressures in the market. Traders will closely monitor further geopolitical developments as they continue to influence the rupee's performance against the greenback.
