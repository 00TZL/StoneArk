Gold (XAU/USD) is holding lower after yesterday’s unsuccessful push above the $4,800 level, showing little momentum to extend losses. Despite the modest uptick in the US dollar, the metal is struggling to find clear direction, with sellers appearing cautious. Trading in the Asian session on Friday keeps gold within its recent familiar range, suggesting that market participants are waiting for fresh catalysts before committing to stronger moves.  

Attention now turns to the upcoming US CPI data, which could provide the necessary impetus for a decisive breakout or breakdown. For now, gold remains in a holding pattern, with bearish momentum subdued and upside attempts capped near key resistance. Traders are clearly in a wait-and-see mode.
