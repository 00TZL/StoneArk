AUD/USD edged higher on Friday, bouncing back from a two-month low near 0.6870 during the Asian session. The recovery allowed the pair to reclaim the 0.6900 level, supported by a softer US Dollar as market participants reassess expectations for upcoming US economic data. Traders noted that short-term technical indicators remain mixed, with the rebound appearing more corrective than a clear shift in trend.  

Despite the modest recovery, the broader bearish sentiment for AUD/USD remains intact. Analysts caution that unless the pair can sustain gains above 0.6920, downside risks persist, potentially revisiting the recent lows. Market attention continues to focus on US Dollar strength and domestic Australian economic developments for direction.
