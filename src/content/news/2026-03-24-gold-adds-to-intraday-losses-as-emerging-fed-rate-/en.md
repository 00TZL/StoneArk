Gold (XAU/USD) extended its intraday losses during Tuesday’s Asian session, struggling to sustain the overnight bounce from a four-month low. The yellow metal hovered near the $4,100 level, closely aligned with its 200-day Simple Moving Average (SMA), a key technical threshold watched by traders. Market participants noted increased selling pressure as investors reacted to persistent strength in the US dollar, underpinned by renewed expectations for further Federal Reserve rate hikes.  

The stronger USD weighed on bullion’s appeal as an alternative asset, capping recovery attempts despite earlier gains. Analysts highlighted that the market remains sensitive to Fed signals, with gold likely to face resistance near $4,120–$4,130 if rate hike sentiment continues to dominate.
