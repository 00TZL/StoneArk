The Indian Rupee (INR) continues to weaken against the US Dollar (USD) on Wednesday, following a sustained rise in oil prices. The currency pair USD/INR extended its gains as rising oil prices add pressure to the rupee, increasing India's import costs and widening the current account deficit.

Oil prices have been climbing further, which typically weighs on the INR due to India's reliance on oil imports. As oil prices rise, concerns about inflation and the trade balance deepen, leading to the continued depreciation of the rupee. This trend adds to the broader market sentiment that favors the US Dollar in the current economic environment.
