> During a two-day summit in Beijing, Chinese President Xi Jinping told U.S. President Donald Trump that recent trade talks yielded “balanced and positive outcomes,” reinforcing cooperation between the two global powers. While promoting economic collaboration, Xi issued a stern warning on Taiwan, calling it the most sensitive issue in U.S.-China relations and cautioning that mishandling it could lead to dangerous conflict. Trump, whose domestic approval has been hurt by the Iran war and inflation, seeks to open Chinese markets further to U.S. businesses, with CEOs like Elon Musk and Jensen Huang accompanying him.

Despite past tensions, both sides are intent on preserving last year’s trade truce and addressing issues such as AI, rare earth supply, and investment mechanisms. China seeks relief from chip equipment export restrictions, while the U.S. aims to boost exports of Boeing planes, farm goods, and energy. Trump also wants China’s help in de-escalating the Iran conflict, though analysts doubt Xi will pressure Tehran. Power dynamics have shifted since Trump’s previous visit, with a now less dominant U.S. position contrasted against China’s growing global influence. A reciprocal visit by Xi to the U.S. is anticipated later in 2026.
