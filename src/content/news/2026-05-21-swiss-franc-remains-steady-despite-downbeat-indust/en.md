The Swiss Franc (CHF) held steady against the US Dollar (USD) on Thursday, showing resilience despite weaker-than-expected economic data. Official figures revealed a notable contraction in Swiss Industrial Production, signaling potential headwinds for the economy. Analysts had anticipated more moderate declines, but the sharper drop underscored challenges in the manufacturing sector, particularly in machinery and chemicals.

Traders appeared largely unmoved, suggesting that the CHF’s safe-haven appeal remains intact amid global market uncertainty. Market participants noted that while industrial output fell, other macro indicators, such as consumer spending and the labor market, continue to support the currency. The CHF/USD pair hovered near previous levels, reflecting a balance between disappointing industrial metrics and broader confidence in Switzerland’s economic stability.
