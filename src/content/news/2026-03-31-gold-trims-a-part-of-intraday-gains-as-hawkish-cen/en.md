Gold (XAU/USD) pulled back from its earlier intraday gains, struggling to hold above the $4,600 level. The precious metal hit a one-and-a-half-week high during Tuesday's Asian session but could not maintain its momentum as hawkish central bank policies weighed on market sentiment. Despite a softer USD supporting gold earlier, rising concerns over aggressive monetary tightening from major central banks capped the upside.

As the day progressed, gold trimmed some of its gains, reflecting the growing influence of central bank actions over the currency markets. The softer USD failed to provide enough support to sustain gold’s rally, leaving it vulnerable to further fluctuations as investors closely monitor global central bank moves.
