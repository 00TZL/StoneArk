Gold (XAU/USD) managed to hold onto modest intraday gains above the $4,750 mark during the Asian session on Wednesday, recovering from significant losses earlier in the week. The precious metal had previously dipped to a one-week low, hovering around the $4,669-$4,668 range, but is now showing signs of resilience amid a softer U.S. dollar.

Despite this rebound, gold lacks bullish conviction, as market participants remain cautious. The recent fluctuations highlight ongoing uncertainty in the market, with traders weighing economic indicators and potential shifts in monetary policy. As the day progresses, investors will be closely monitoring any developments that could influence gold's trajectory in the near term.
