The AUD/USD pair extended losses on Thursday, slipping 0.7% to around 0.6870 during late Asian trading. Traders cited renewed risk-off sentiment weighing on the Australian Dollar, which struggled against the U.S. Dollar amid cautious market sentiment. The move comes as investors digest global economic uncertainties, prompting a flight to safer assets and putting pressure on higher-yielding currencies like the AUD.  

Analysts suggest that the downside momentum could intensify if the pair breaches key support near 0.6830. Market watchers note that persistent selling pressure may continue as risk appetite remains fragile, keeping the Australian Dollar on the defensive. Immediate focus is on whether the AUD/USD can stabilize above current levels or slide further in the near term.
