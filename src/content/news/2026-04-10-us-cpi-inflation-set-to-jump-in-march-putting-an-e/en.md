US CPI inflation is set to surge in March, ending a two-year period of gradual decline. The US Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) will release the Consumer Price Index (CPI) data this Friday, and expectations are for a notable jump in inflation. This increase is primarily attributed to the recent spike in crude oil prices following the US and Israel’s joint military action against Iran.

The rising oil prices have already begun to influence the broader economy, leading to higher costs across various sectors. Analysts are closely monitoring this CPI report as it may signal a shift in inflationary trends, possibly prompting a reevaluation of monetary policy by the Federal Reserve.
