Commerzbank reports that the Thai baht remained under pressure after the Bank of Thailand (BoT) held its policy rate steady at 1.0%, reinforcing a cautious policy stance. Policymakers continue to prioritize growth risks over inflation concerns, signaling limited urgency for tightening. The tone has been interpreted as dovish by markets, contributing to sustained weakness in the currency.

USD/THB extended its upward momentum, rising for a fifth consecutive session as policy divergence and cautious BoT messaging weighed on sentiment. The pair climbed 0.9% to 33.43, reflecting steady dollar demand and softer regional risk appetite. Analysts note that the combination of stable Thai rates and resilient US yields continues to support near-term upside pressure on the pair in the near term outlook.
