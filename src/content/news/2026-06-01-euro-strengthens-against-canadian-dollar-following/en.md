The Euro strengthened against the Canadian Dollar for a second consecutive session on Monday, with EUR/CAD climbing to around 1.6110 during European trading hours. The currency pair remained supported after the release of Germany’s latest Retail Sales report, which showed consumer spending was more resilient than expected.

German Retail Sales declined by 0.3% month-on-month in April, outperforming market expectations for a 0.4% decrease. The smaller-than-forecast contraction provided support for the Euro, reinforcing confidence in the Eurozone’s largest economy. As a result, EUR/CAD extended its upward momentum, while traders continued to assess the implications of the data for the region’s economic outlook and monetary policy expectations.
