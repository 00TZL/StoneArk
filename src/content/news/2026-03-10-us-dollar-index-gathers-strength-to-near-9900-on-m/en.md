The US Dollar Index (DXY) is edging closer to the 99.00 mark, trading near 98.90 in the early European session on Tuesday. The index, which measures the value of the US Dollar against a basket of six major currencies, is finding support amid ongoing geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. These risks are contributing to a flight to safety, boosting demand for the dollar as investors seek a stable haven.

As the market focuses on the geopolitical climate, all eyes will soon turn to the US Consumer Price Index (CPI) data, set to be released later in the week. The outcome of this crucial economic indicator could drive further movement in the DXY, adding to the already volatile trading environment.
