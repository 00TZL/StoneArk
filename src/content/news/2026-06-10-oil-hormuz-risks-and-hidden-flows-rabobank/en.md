Oil prices eased after the US energy secretary said traffic through the Strait of Hormuz has been increasing, helping to ease immediate concerns over disruptions to one of the world’s most important energy chokepoints. The comments suggested that crude flows remain resilient despite ongoing geopolitical risks in the region.

Rabobank Global Strategist Michael Every noted that there is also evidence indicating the US Navy may be facilitating greater crude movements through Hormuz than official shipping data currently reflect. If confirmed, these hidden flows could mean actual oil transit volumes are stronger than reported, potentially reducing near-term supply fears even as traders continue to monitor regional tensions closely.
