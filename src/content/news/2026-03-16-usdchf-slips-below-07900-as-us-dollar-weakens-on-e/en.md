USD/CHF dipped below the 0.7900 mark, trading near 0.7890 in early Asian session on Monday, following four consecutive days of gains. The US Dollar faced broad-based losses as investor sentiment improved, pulling back from safe-haven demand that had supported the greenback in recent sessions. Traders appeared to react to easing geopolitical tensions, which weighed on the appeal of the USD as a refuge.  

Market participants were encouraged by reports suggesting the United States may announce a coalition to escort vessels through the Strait of Hormuz. This development eased concerns over potential disruptions in global oil supplies, reducing risk aversion and prompting flows back into higher-yielding currencies. As a result, USD/CHF gave up some of its recent gains.
