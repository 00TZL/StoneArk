The British Pound attracted support alongside the Japanese Yen as underperformance in global bond markets triggered fixed income rebalancing flows, according to BNY strategist Geoff Yu. He noted that steepening G10 yield curves and growing stagflation concerns are reshaping investor positioning across major currencies.

Yu said the move reflects broader caution in rates markets, where longer-dated bonds are weakening faster amid inflation risks and slower growth expectations. In this environment, Sterling and the Yen are benefiting from portfolio adjustments tied to fixed income exposure, with investors seeking relative stability as volatility in global bond markets increases.
