The NZD/USD pair found support near 0.5680, a level that marks a four-month low, during the Asian session on Monday. Buyers stepped in, helping the pair recover and halt a two-day losing streak. This rebound came as the US dollar faced downward pressure, primarily influenced by ongoing ceasefire discussions that are reducing demand for the greenback as a safe-haven currency.

As a result, the pair regained the 0.5700 level, reflecting a modest recovery. The sentiment around the ceasefire talks continues to weigh on the USD, creating an opportunity for the New Zealand dollar to gain some traction. Market participants will likely keep an eye on further developments in the ceasefire negotiations, which could impact USD strength in the short term.
