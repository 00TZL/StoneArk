Germany’s ZEW Economic Sentiment surprised markets in June, rising to 10.5 and returning to positive territory after staying negative in May. The indicator beat expectations of -6.0 and improved sharply from -10.2 previously, signaling a stronger outlook for the eurozone’s largest economy amid easing financial stress and improving investor confidence.

For EUR/USD, the upside surprise supports the euro by reinforcing expectations of improving economic momentum in Germany, potentially reducing recession fears across the eurozone. Traders may interpret the rebound as mildly bullish for risk sentiment, though sustained gains will depend on upcoming inflation and ECB signals that could shape interest rate expectations and market positioning near key technical levels. Volatility may increase ahead of next data releases in Europe cycle.
