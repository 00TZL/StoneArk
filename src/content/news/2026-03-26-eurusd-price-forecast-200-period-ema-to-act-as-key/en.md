The EUR/USD pair edged slightly higher on Thursday, hovering near 1.1570 in late Asian trading. Investors appear cautious as tensions in the Middle East continue to influence market sentiment, prompting a wait-and-see approach. The euro’s modest gains come amid mixed economic signals from the eurozone and the U.S., keeping the pair within a narrow range. Traders are closely monitoring geopolitical developments, which could dictate short-term volatility and risk appetite across currency markets.  

Technical analysis highlights the 200-period EMA as a significant hurdle for EUR/USD, likely serving as resistance in the near term. Breaking above this level may open the door for further upside, while failure to surpass it could reinforce consolidation around current levels, leaving the pair sensitive to both economic releases and geopolitical news.
