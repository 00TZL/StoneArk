Gold (XAU/USD) continued its recovery on Tuesday, extending the overnight rebound from the $5,000 psychological level. The precious metal saw increased buying interest during the Asian session, as geopolitical risks provided continued support for safe-haven demand. Concerns surrounding global instability kept investors focused on gold as a secure asset, driving its price higher.

However, gains were capped by the strength of the US dollar. Despite the broader market’s risk aversion, the greenback's solid performance limited gold's upside potential, as a stronger USD makes the metal more expensive for holders of other currencies. This kept gold's movements relatively constrained, with traders balancing safe-haven demand against the dollar's strength.
