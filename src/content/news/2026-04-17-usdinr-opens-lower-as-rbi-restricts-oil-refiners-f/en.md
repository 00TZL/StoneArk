USD/INR started the session on a weaker note on Friday, with the Indian Rupee gaining ground against the US Dollar. The move comes after the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) barred oil refiners from purchasing dollars in the spot market, prompting the central bank to open special credit lines for state-run oil companies to cover their foreign exchange requirements. This measure has provided immediate support to the INR, reflecting reduced demand pressure for dollars from the energy sector.  

Market participants noted that the RBI’s intervention aims to manage currency volatility while ensuring liquidity for essential imports. The Rupee’s early gains highlight investor confidence in the central bank’s measures, even as global USD strength continues to influence regional forex trends.
