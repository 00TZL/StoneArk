The US Dollar Index (DXY), tracking the greenback against six major peers, held steady near the 98.00 level in Thursday’s Asian session, following a near 0.5% drop in the previous trading day. Traders appeared cautious as demand for safe-haven assets eased amid growing optimism over US-Iran developments, tempering the dollar’s recent gains. The index’s stability reflects a pause after a period of heightened volatility, with investors digesting geopolitical news and weighing its potential impact on global markets.  

Currency markets showed mixed reactions, with the euro and yen edging higher as the dollar cooled slightly. Analysts noted that while short-term risk sentiment is improving, the DXY’s positioning suggests any strong rebound could be limited unless fresh economic data or policy signals emerge.
