Gold prices slipped from a near two-week high on Wednesday, giving back earlier Asian session gains as investors weighed the impact of U.S. monetary policy. The yellow metal, which had climbed to a short-term peak amid subdued risk appetite, is now trading near the lower end of its daily range, staying below the $4,700 level. Market participants remain cautious as safe-haven demand competes with rising U.S. dollar strength.  

The U.S. dollar has found support from expectations of further Federal Reserve rate hikes, putting pressure on non-yielding assets like gold. Traders are closely watching economic data and Fed signals, with any indication of tighter policy likely to cap gold’s upside in the near term. Volatility is expected to persist around key psychological levels.
