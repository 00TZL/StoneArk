Oil prices remain sensitive to geopolitical developments after Rabobank strategist Michael Every said US efforts to secure crude flows through the Strait of Hormuz under an emerging US-Iran memorandum of understanding (MoU) were reportedly blocked by Saudi Arabia. The setback has heightened concerns over the security of one of the world's most important oil shipping routes.

For forex markets, renewed uncertainty around Hormuz raises the risk of higher energy prices, supporting safe-haven demand while weighing on currencies linked to oil-importing economies. Investors are closely monitoring developments between Washington, Tehran, and regional powers, as any further deterioration in negotiations could increase volatility across commodity and currency markets.
