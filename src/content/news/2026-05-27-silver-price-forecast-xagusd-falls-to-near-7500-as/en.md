Silver prices extended losses for a second straight session on Wednesday, with XAG/USD trading near $75.10 per troy ounce during early European trading. The metal remained under pressure as fading optimism over easing tensions between the United States and Iran reduced demand for safe-haven assets, prompting traders to trim bullish positions in silver.

Market sentiment shifted after hopes for diplomatic progress between Washington and Tehran weakened, limiting support for precious metals. Investors also stayed cautious ahead of key economic signals and broader risk sentiment in global markets. With geopolitical concerns easing slightly, silver struggled to regain momentum, keeping XAG/USD close to the $75.00 psychological level despite ongoing uncertainty across financial markets.
