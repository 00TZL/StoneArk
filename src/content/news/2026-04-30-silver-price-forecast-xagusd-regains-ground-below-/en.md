Silver (XAG/USD) has reclaimed some ground, rising 1.5% to around $72.35 during Thursday's European trading session. The precious metal bounced back after hitting a fresh three-week low of $70.86 on Wednesday, which was driven by concerns over the Federal Reserve's hawkish stance.

Despite the Fed's tightening bias and its implications for the broader markets, silver found support and is currently recovering its losses. The rebound comes as traders digest the Fed’s recent remarks, weighing their potential impact on the precious metals market. The outlook for silver remains sensitive to further monetary policy signals and shifts in investor sentiment.
