The Indian Rupee (INR) strengthened at the opening of Wednesday's trading session, reversing previous losses against the US Dollar (USD). This rise follows a public holiday on Tuesday, marking the Shri Mahavir Jayanti. The INR opened on a positive note as market sentiment improved globally, with investors reacting to the easing tensions in the Middle East.

The de-escalation of the ongoing conflict in the region provided relief across global markets, supporting riskier assets like emerging market currencies. As a result, the Indian Rupee gained ground against the USD early in the session. Traders remain cautious, though, as geopolitical risks continue to influence currency movements.
