Gold (XAU/USD) faces significant selling pressure as a new week begins, dipping to a four-day low in the Asian session. The precious metal is weighed down by rising U.S. dollar strength, driven by persistent inflationary concerns. Despite earlier support, gold struggles to maintain its ground as the greenback remains resilient.

The decline in gold prices comes despite ongoing geopolitical risks, which have traditionally offered some support for the metal. However, the inflation-driven surge in the USD has overshadowed these concerns, pushing XAU/USD closer to key psychological levels. While gold attempts to stabilize, market sentiment remains cautious with the price hovering just above the $5,000 mark.
