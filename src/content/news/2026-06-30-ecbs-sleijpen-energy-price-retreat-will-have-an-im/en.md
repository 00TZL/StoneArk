European Central Bank policymaker and Dutch central bank chief Olaf Sleijpen said in European trade on Tuesday that recent declines in energy prices are expected to weigh on inflation across the euro area. He noted that easing geopolitical tensions in the Middle East have helped drive energy costs lower, strengthening expectations of softer price pressures in the near term overall.

Market participants interpreted the remarks as reinforcing expectations that disinflationary forces may persist, particularly if energy markets remain subdued. Traders in euro-related assets adjusted short-term inflation outlooks, while some analysts suggested the ECB could gain additional room to maintain a cautious policy stance. However, policymakers continue to monitor broader services inflation and wage growth dynamics across the bloc overall tone.
