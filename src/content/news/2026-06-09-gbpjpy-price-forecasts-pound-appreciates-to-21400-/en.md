GBP/JPY advanced to 214.00 on Tuesday as the British Pound posted moderate gains against the Japanese Yen, supported by a modest improvement in global risk sentiment. The pair attracted buying interest during the session, extending its recovery and reflecting reduced demand for the safe-haven yen as market confidence improved.

Despite the move higher, the outlook remains fragile and the cross is still vulnerable to downside pressure. Traders continue to monitor broader risk conditions and expectations surrounding monetary policy, which could influence demand for both currencies. While GBP/JPY is holding near 214.00, a deterioration in sentiment or renewed yen strength could limit further gains and expose the pair to a corrective pullback in the near term.
