The Canadian Dollar has trimmed some of its earlier losses against the US Dollar as rising oil prices provide support, offsetting expectations of a hawkish Federal Reserve. The USD/CAD pair has pulled back from its earlier Asian session high, now trading in the 1.3860-1.3855 range, up by approximately 0.15% on the day.

Oil prices have been a key factor in the loonie’s recovery, as the commodity is a major export for Canada. Meanwhile, market sentiment remains cautious amid the Fed's aggressive stance on interest rate hikes, which continues to drive volatility in the currency markets. Traders will likely keep an eye on oil movements for further direction.
