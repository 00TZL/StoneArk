The USD/CHF pair climbed above the 0.7800 mark, reaching around 0.7820 in early European trading on Thursday. Market participants are eyeing the US Dollar as it gains ground against the Swiss Franc, driven by rising oil prices that could stoke inflationary pressures. Investors anticipate that the US Federal Reserve may respond with a more hawkish approach to monetary policy, keeping the dollar supported.  

Traders remain cautious but upbeat, focusing on the potential implications of sustained inflation for interest rate expectations. The Swiss Franc, traditionally seen as a safe-haven currency, faces headwinds amid renewed dollar strength. Overall, the USD/CHF outlook appears bullish in the near term as markets price in a firming Fed stance against a backdrop of energy-driven inflation concerns.
