The Indian Rupee maintained its gains against the US Dollar on Wednesday, supported by a softer greenback and easing crude oil prices. During Asian trading hours, USD/INR continued its decline for the second consecutive day, hovering around the 95.00 mark. Market participants cited the subdued demand for the dollar and lower energy costs as key factors cushioning the rupee’s advance.  

Investors are now turning their attention to India’s economic data scheduled for release later today, including the HSBC Composite and Services Purchasing Managers' Index (PMI). Analysts suggest that the readings could influence short-term rupee sentiment, with stronger-than-expected PMI numbers likely to provide additional support for the local currency, while weaker figures might pressure it back toward recent highs.
