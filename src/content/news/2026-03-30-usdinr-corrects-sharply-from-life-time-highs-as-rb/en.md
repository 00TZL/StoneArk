The Indian Rupee (INR) staged a strong recovery against the US Dollar (USD) in early trading Monday, pulling back from record highs. The USD/INR pair dropped more than 1%, approaching the 94.00 level, as investors reacted to fresh regulatory measures from the Reserve Bank of India (RBI). The central bank’s move to cap banks’ foreign-exchange exposure aims to curb excessive demand for dollars and stabilize the domestic currency amid recent volatility.  

Market participants welcomed the intervention, which prompted renewed buying of the rupee and eased pressure on USD/INR. Analysts noted that while global dollar strength remains a factor, the RBI’s limits could provide near-term support for the rupee, signaling potential moderation in currency swings going forward.
