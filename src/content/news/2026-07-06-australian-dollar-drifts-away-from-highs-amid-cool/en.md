Australian Dollar (AUD) is trading moderately lower against the US Dollar (USD) on Monday, retreating from recent highs as softer domestic inflation data eases expectations for further Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) tightening.

Sentiment around the Australian currency was further pressured by weak employment figures, reinforcing views that the RBA may maintain a cautious stance in upcoming policy meetings. Investors are reassessing rate-cut timing as economic momentum shows signs of cooling across key indicators. The combination of easing inflation and softer labor conditions has reduced demand for the AUD, keeping it under pressure against the greenback. Market participants will closely watch upcoming RBA commentary and labor revisions for further direction in the currency outlook over the near-term trading horizon period.
