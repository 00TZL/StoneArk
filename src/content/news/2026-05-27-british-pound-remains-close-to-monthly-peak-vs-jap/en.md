The British Pound stayed near a one-month high against the Japanese Yen on Wednesday, as renewed Middle East tensions continued to support demand for risk-sensitive currency pairs. GBP/JPY attracted fresh dip-buying after easing slightly from the 214.65-214.70 zone, which marked a fresh monthly peak reached in the previous session.

The cross maintained modest intraday gains during early European trading, with traders showing limited interest in aggressive bearish positions. Market sentiment remained relatively stable despite geopolitical uncertainty, helping the Pound hold firm versus the safe-haven Yen. Investors are also monitoring broader risk trends and upcoming economic signals for fresh direction in the pair.
