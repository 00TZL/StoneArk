West Texas Intermediate (WTI) oil rebounded on Thursday, holding gains near $92.00 per barrel as tensions in the Strait of Hormuz continue to restrict supply. After slipping for two consecutive sessions, WTI climbed to around $91.90 during Asian trading, supported by ongoing geopolitical uncertainties and strong demand expectations. Traders are closely watching the narrow shipping corridor, a key passage for global crude, as any disruption could tighten the market further.  

Market participants noted that the recent rebound reflects both supply-side concerns and technical buying after the recent pullback. Analysts suggest that if the Strait remains constrained, WTI could sustain its current levels, though broader market dynamics, including U.S. inventory data and economic signals, will continue to influence near-term price movements.
