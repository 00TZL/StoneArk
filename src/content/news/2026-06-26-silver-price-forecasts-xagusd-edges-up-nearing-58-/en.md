Silver (XAG/USD) edged higher on Friday, rebounding from seven-month lows below $56.00 as the US Dollar took a breather across markets. The white metal recovered momentum during the session, attracting dip-buying interest after recent weakness. Price action reflected improved sentiment, with traders reassessing dollar strength and positioning ahead of key macroeconomic signals.

At the time of writing, XAG/USD reached session highs at $57.80, extending its intraday rebound from earlier lows. The move suggests short-term bullish momentum as the market reacts to a softer US Dollar tone. However, traders remain cautious, monitoring broader macro signals and potential volatility, which could influence whether silver sustains gains or consolidates within its recent trading range into the next trading sessions ahead on positioning flows.
