USD/JPY found some footing near 157 as Japanese authorities reportedly stepped in after the pair surged briefly to 160.72, according to Commerzbank’s Volkmar Baur. The intervention comes amid heightened market sensitivity to the yen, which has faced persistent pressure in recent weeks. Traders are watching the Bank of Japan closely for signs that policy might shift, but the authorities’ move underscores their readiness to defend the currency against rapid depreciation.  

Tokyo’s latest inflation figures add further context: headline CPI gains were driven entirely by energy costs, while core inflation fell to a one-year low, highlighting weak underlying price pressures. This combination of intervention and subdued core inflation has helped cap yen losses against the dollar, providing a temporary floor around 157.
