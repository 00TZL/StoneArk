WTI Crude Oil prices are facing challenges around the $82.00 level, struggling to extend the rebound seen earlier this week from the sub-$76.00 lows. During the Asian session on Wednesday, the commodity encountered fresh selling pressure, pushing it nearly 4% lower on the day. The drop comes as market participants weigh ongoing geopolitical tensions, including the Iran conflict, which continues to add uncertainty to oil markets.

The key support for bulls lies near the 200-hour moving average, which is seen as a critical level for any potential upside recovery. If WTI can hold above this technical threshold, there could be a chance to reclaim the $82.00 handle and attempt further gains. However, without a significant shift in sentiment, the outlook remains uncertain.
