Indian Rupee (INR) trades strongly against the US Dollar (USD) during Monday afternoon trading in India. The USD/INR pair plunges to near 94.60 as oil prices nosedive following the announcement of a permanent peace deal between the United States and Iran.

Market sentiment improves for the Indian currency as the sharp decline in crude oil prices eases concerns over import costs, supporting the rupee's gains against the dollar. Traders note that expectations of stable Middle East conditions are driving safe-haven flows away from the greenback. However, analysts caution that volatility may persist if geopolitical headlines shift or oil markets rebound, keeping USD/INR sensitive around the 94.60 level in the near term as traders watch fresh macro cues closely ahead globally.
