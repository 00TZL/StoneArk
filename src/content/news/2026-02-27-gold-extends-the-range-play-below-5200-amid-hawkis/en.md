Gold (XAU/USD) continues to trade within a narrow range below the $5,200 level, extending its consolidative pattern during the Asian session on Friday. The precious metal struggles to capitalize on the modest gains seen over the past two days as hawkish signals from the Federal Reserve weigh on investor sentiment.

Geopolitical tensions further complicate the outlook for gold, with significant American naval and air power deployments in the Middle East raising concerns. These developments contribute to market uncertainty, influencing gold's performance as traders assess the potential implications for risk assets and safe-haven demand.
