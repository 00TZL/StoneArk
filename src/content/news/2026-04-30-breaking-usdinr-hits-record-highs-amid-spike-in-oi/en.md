The Indian Rupee (INR) tumbled to a record low against the US Dollar (USD) in early Thursday trading, as the currency faced mounting pressure from surging global oil prices and persistent US monetary tightening. USD/INR opened sharply higher, reflecting investor concerns over India’s widening import bill and the impact of elevated crude costs on inflation. Market participants also cited risk-off sentiment fueling demand for the dollar, as traders reassess global growth prospects in light of rising energy expenses.  

Adding to the pressure, the Federal Reserve maintained its hawkish stance, keeping interest rates elevated and signaling continued vigilance against inflation. The combination of stronger USD momentum and domestic macro pressures has pushed USD/INR to unprecedented levels, intensifying focus on policy response from the Reserve Bank of India.
