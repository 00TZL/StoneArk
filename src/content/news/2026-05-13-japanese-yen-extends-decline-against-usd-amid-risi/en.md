The Japanese Yen continued its slide against the US Dollar on Wednesday, with the USD/JPY pair climbing to around 157.70 during the European session. Market participants are increasingly pricing in a more hawkish stance from the Federal Reserve, fueling demand for the dollar. Investors are weighing the impact of potential interest rate hikes in the US, which tends to support the greenback while putting pressure on currencies like the yen.  

Traders also cite risk sentiment and yield differentials as factors keeping the yen under pressure. The weakening of JPY comes despite domestic indicators showing some resilience, highlighting the influence of global monetary expectations. Analysts suggest that unless the Bank of Japan signals a shift in its ultra-loose policy, the yen could continue to face downward pressure against the dollar.
