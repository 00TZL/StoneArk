The Pound Sterling (GBP) extends its gains against the US Dollar (USD) in early European trading on Monday, with GBP/USD climbing toward 1.3600. The movement is supported by hawkish signals from the Bank of England (BoE), suggesting a possible shift towards higher interest rates later this year. This has bolstered investor confidence in the GBP, pushing the currency higher against the greenback.

However, ongoing tensions in the Strait of Hormuz are limiting further upside potential for the Pound. The geopolitical concerns in the region are creating a cautious atmosphere in global markets, keeping traders from making more aggressive bets on the GBP’s upward momentum despite the BoE’s hawkish stance.
