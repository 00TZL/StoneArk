Gold (XAU/USD) bounced back during the Asian session on Friday, recovering to around $4,735 after a seven-day losing streak. The precious metal saw a modest uptick, driven by increasing demand for safe-haven assets as tensions in the Middle East, particularly between the US and Iran, escalated. 

The heightened geopolitical uncertainty is fueling concerns, pushing traders to seek safer investments like gold. Market participants are now keenly watching developments in the region, as any further escalation could lead to more substantial moves in gold prices.
