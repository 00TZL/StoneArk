The Indian Rupee (INR) continued its decline against the US Dollar (USD) on Tuesday, extending the recent rally in USD/INR. After a brief pause in the opening session, the pair surged toward 94.46, reflecting persistent pressure on the local currency. Traders point to rising global crude oil prices as a key factor, as India remains heavily dependent on oil imports, which exacerbates the current trade deficit and fuels demand for the dollar.  

Market participants remain cautious, with the Rupee facing headwinds from external factors alongside domestic inflation concerns. Analysts suggest that unless oil prices ease or capital inflows improve, the USD/INR may see further gains in the near term, keeping the Indian currency under continued strain.
