The Japanese Yen is edging higher against the US Dollar amid growing fears of intervention by Japan's authorities. The USD/JPY pair saw some selling pressure during the Asian session on Friday, pulling back from its recent highs. The pair currently trades near 159.50, a level not seen since July 2024, following a three-day winning streak.

This dip in the USD/JPY pair comes as the US Dollar softens slightly, allowing the Yen to recover some ground. Market participants are keeping an eye on potential intervention by Japan’s central bank, which has been a concern as the Yen continues to weaken. The current price action highlights the ongoing volatility in the currency pair.
