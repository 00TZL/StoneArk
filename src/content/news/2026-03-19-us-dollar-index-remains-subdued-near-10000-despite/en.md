The US Dollar Index (DXY) remains subdued near the 100.00 mark on Thursday, hovering around 100.10 during Asian trading hours. This follows a strong rebound in the previous session, where the index gained almost 0.75%, reflecting temporary optimism around the greenback. Traders appear cautious despite a generally hawkish outlook from the Federal Reserve, weighing concerns over global growth and ongoing market volatility.  

Market participants are closely monitoring how the dollar reacts to mixed economic signals and Fed policy expectations. The index’s muted performance suggests that while the central bank’s stance supports the USD, broader risk sentiment and technical pressures are limiting further gains. Analysts note that movements around key psychological levels like 100.00 could shape near-term trading dynamics.
