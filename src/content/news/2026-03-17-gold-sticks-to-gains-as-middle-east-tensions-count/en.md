Gold (XAU/USD) maintains modest intraday gains during the Asian session on Tuesday, holding steady despite a lack of strong bullish momentum. The precious metal remains below the $1,050 level, as investors weigh geopolitical risks against shifting monetary policy expectations. Although the market has priced in fewer Federal Reserve rate cuts, the uncertainty in the Middle East continues to offer support to gold.

Tensions in the region, particularly the ongoing conflict between Israel and Hezbollah in Lebanon, have further fueled concerns about instability. The war between the US-backed Israel and Iran shows no clear signs of de-escalating anytime soon, keeping demand for safe-haven assets like gold elevated. These factors have kept gold prices steady despite reduced bets on aggressive Fed rate cuts.
