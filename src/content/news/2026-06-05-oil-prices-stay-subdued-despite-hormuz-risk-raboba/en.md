Oil prices remain muted despite escalating geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, according to Rabobank. Senior Market Strategist Benjamin Picton highlighted that Brent futures dropped sharply, even as the Strait of Hormuz faces a potential closure scenario in their baseline outlook. Traders appear cautious, weighing supply disruption risks against softer demand signals, which has kept price movements relatively subdued.  

Picton emphasized that market participants are closely monitoring developments around the Hormuz chokepoint, yet the usual risk premium hasn’t fully translated into higher oil prices. The strategist noted that while geopolitical pressures typically drive spikes, current market sentiment reflects a balance between uncertainty in supply and existing global inventories, keeping Brent futures under pressure.
