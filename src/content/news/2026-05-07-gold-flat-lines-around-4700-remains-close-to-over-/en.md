Gold (XAU/USD) held steady around the $4,700 level on Thursday, maintaining proximity to a more than one-week high as the U.S. dollar showed signs of weakness. The metal has found support following a two-day rebound from the $4,500 area, which marked its lowest point in over a month. Market participants remained cautious, with gold hovering in a narrow range amid light Asian session trading.  

Despite the recent uptick, bulls have struggled to extend gains, as broader market sentiment and lingering uncertainty around the dollar continue to cap momentum. Analysts note that while the short-term outlook appears balanced, any further softening in the USD could give gold room to test higher levels in the near term.
