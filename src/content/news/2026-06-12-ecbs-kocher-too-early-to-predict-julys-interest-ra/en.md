European Central Bank (ECB) Governing Council member Martin Kocher commented in European trading on Friday that it remains too early to anticipate the bank’s decision on interest rates for the July meeting. Kocher emphasized that market speculation should be tempered, as economic data and inflation trends will play a critical role in guiding the ECB’s next move.

Traders are watching the euro closely following his remarks, as uncertainty around monetary policy could affect short-term currency volatility. Analysts note that while the ECB has been adjusting rates to manage inflation, Kocher’s statement underscores that no pre-commitment has been made, leaving the euro susceptible to market swings ahead of the upcoming policy meeting.
