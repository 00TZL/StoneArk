The Indian Rupee (INR) stayed under pressure against the US Dollar (USD) on Wednesday, hovering near record lows as the USD/INR pair remained close to the 97.00 mark. Persistent strength in the US Dollar, combined with rising US Treasury yields, continued to weigh on the local currency and kept market sentiment cautious.

The Rupee also faced renewed downside pressure from elevated crude oil prices, driven by concerns that the Strait of Hormuz could remain disrupted for an extended period. As India relies heavily on imported energy, higher oil costs are seen widening the trade deficit and increasing inflation risks, adding to bearish sentiment surrounding the INR.
