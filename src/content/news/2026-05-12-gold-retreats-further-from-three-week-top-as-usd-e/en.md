Gold (XAU/USD) retreated from its three-week peak during Tuesday’s Asian session, easing back toward the $4,700 level. The pullback comes as the US dollar gained modest ground, pressuring safe-haven demand for bullion. Traders appeared cautious, unwinding some positions after the recent surge that lifted gold to its highest since early April. Market participants are also assessing broader risk sentiment and positioning ahead of major US economic data releases.

Focus is now on the upcoming US consumer price index (CPI) report, which could significantly influence both gold and the dollar. Analysts suggest that any surprises in inflation could drive volatility, with gold potentially regaining upward momentum if inflation remains elevated or retreating further if the dollar strengthens. Investors are weighing hedging strategies, keeping a close eye on real-time data for cues on the next move in precious metals.
