AUD/JPY hovered near the 111.00 level, trading around 110.90 during Thursday’s Asian session, as the cross steadied after three consecutive days of losses. The pair moved sideways, with price action showing limited momentum while investors digested fresh cues from Australia and broader market sentiment.

Support for the Australian Dollar came after comments from RBA Assistant Governor Kent, which helped underpin the currency and prevented deeper declines in the cross. His remarks provided a modest boost to AUD, keeping AUD/JPY anchored despite cautious risk appetite. However, gains remained capped as traders awaited further macroeconomic signals and developments influencing both the yen and overall risk sentiment in the near term amid subdued volatility conditions across Asian trading hours on Thursday morning session.
