EUR/GBP edges lower towards the 0.8700 mark during the early European session on Wednesday, reflecting a slight pullback in the cross. A two-week ceasefire agreement between the United States and Iran has lifted global risk sentiment, encouraging a shift towards riskier assets. This has generally benefitted the British Pound (GBP), as investors seek higher yields, reducing demand for the Euro (EUR).

The European Central Bank’s hawkish stance continues to provide some cushion against further downside for the EUR. Despite the Pound gaining traction, the ECB's tightening policy expectations lend support to the single currency, limiting any significant decline in the EUR/GBP pair. Market focus remains on how geopolitical developments and central bank actions will shape currency movements.
