The Indian rupee strengthened this week, with USD/INR retreating 0.5% to 95.71, reflecting supportive actions from the Reserve Bank of India (RBI). Analysts at Commerzbank note that the currency is also down 0.3% on the week, signaling sustained market confidence. The RBI’s active intervention in spot markets, alongside tighter regulations on gold imports, has helped curb volatility and bolstered the rupee amid broader USD strength.  

Market watchers say the central bank’s stance, paired with ongoing discussions on domestic interest rates, underpins investor sentiment. The combination of policy measures and rate talk is keeping USD/INR subdued, even as global currency movements remain fluid. Traders will likely monitor further RBI guidance for cues on near-term rupee direction.
