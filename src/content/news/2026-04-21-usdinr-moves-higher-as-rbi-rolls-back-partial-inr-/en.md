The Indian Rupee (INR) opened lower against the US Dollar (USD) on Tuesday, reflecting market reactions to recent policy adjustments. The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has rolled back some of its partial measures aimed at supporting the INR, contributing to the currency's weakness. As a result, the USD/INR pair has experienced upward momentum, indicating a shift in trader sentiment.

This move by the RBI has raised concerns about the stability of the rupee, prompting investors to reassess their positions. Market participants will be closely monitoring further developments from the central bank and any potential economic indicators that could influence the INR's trajectory in the coming days. The current exchange rate reflects the ongoing volatility in the currency market.
