Gold (XAU/USD) remained trapped in a narrow range above the $4,300 level on Wednesday, struggling to build meaningful upside momentum. The precious metal stayed under mild pressure during the first half of the European session as investors refrained from taking aggressive positions ahead of the Federal Reserve’s policy announcement. Market participants largely adopted a wait-and-see approach, keeping price action subdued despite ongoing support from broader safe-haven demand.

Attention is now firmly focused on the Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) rate decision, which is expected to provide fresh direction for gold prices. Traders will closely monitor the Fed’s interest rate outlook, policy statement and guidance on future easing prospects. Any shift in expectations regarding the timing or pace of rate cuts could trigger stronger volatility and determine gold’s next move beyond its recent consolidation range.
