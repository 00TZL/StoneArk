Gold (XAU/USD) extended its losses on Monday, slipping below the $4,300 level for the first time since March 23. Trading in the European session saw the metal maintain a bearish tone as investors reacted to ongoing inflation concerns, which continue to strengthen expectations for further Federal Reserve rate hikes. The precious metal struggled to find support amid firm Treasury yields, keeping gold under pressure throughout the morning.

Analysts note that persistent inflation fears are driving demand for higher interest rates, a factor typically weighing on non-yielding assets like gold. With XAU/USD hovering just under $4,300, market participants are closely watching U.S. economic data for clues on the Fed’s next move. Any signs of elevated inflation could push gold lower, while softer data might offer temporary relief.
