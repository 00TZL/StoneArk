European Central Bank (ECB) policymaker Dimitar Radev stated on Tuesday that it is too soon to determine whether a rate hike will be necessary during the ECB's upcoming policy meeting in April. His comments highlight the uncertainty surrounding the central bank's next move amid ongoing economic conditions.

Radev emphasized that more data and developments would be needed to assess whether tightening monetary policy would be appropriate at the time. Investors and market participants are closely watching the ECB's decisions, with expectations of further guidance on the future direction of interest rates as the April meeting approaches.
