The USD/INR currency pair continues its upward trend as concerns grow over a potential prolonged closure of the Hormuz Strait. This geopolitical tension has fueled demand for the US dollar, pushing the Indian Rupee (INR) lower for the fifth consecutive trading day. Market sentiment remains cautious as investors assess the implications of rising oil prices and potential disruptions in global trade.

On Friday, the INR opened on a stronger note against the USD, but the overall outlook remains challenging for the local currency. Traders are closely monitoring developments in the Middle East, as any escalation could further impact the INR's performance. As the market reacts to these geopolitical risks, volatility is expected to persist in the USD/INR pair.
