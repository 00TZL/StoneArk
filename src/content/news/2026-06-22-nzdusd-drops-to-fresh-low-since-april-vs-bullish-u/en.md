NZD/USD extends losses for a fourth consecutive day, marking six declines in the past seven sessions, as the pair slides to its lowest level since April 8 during Monday’s European session. The move reflects broad US dollar strength and cautious risk sentiment across FX markets.

Hawkish Federal Reserve expectations continue to underpin the US dollar, with traders scaling back bets on near-term rate cuts amid resilient US economic data and persistent inflation concerns. At the same time, geopolitical uncertainty surrounding Iran adds to broader risk aversion, further pressuring higher-beta currencies like the New Zealand dollar. Market participants will now watch upcoming US macro releases and Fed commentary for fresh directional cues in FX markets this week ahead for overall signals.
