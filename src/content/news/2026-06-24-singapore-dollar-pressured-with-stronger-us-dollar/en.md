Singapore Dollar remains under pressure as the US Dollar extends its broader advance, with Commerzbank noting continued weakness in Asian FX. USD/SGD rose 0.3% to 1.2970, reflecting persistent demand for the greenback. The pair has been grinding higher since mid-June, tracking global dollar strength and firmer US yields.

According to Commerzbank, the move reflects a broader USD bid driven by resilient US macro data and cautious risk sentiment, which continues to weigh on regional currencies. Analysts expect USD/SGD to remain supported in the near term if dollar strength persists, though momentum may slow if US yields stabilize. Traders will watch upcoming US data releases and Fed commentary for further direction in the currency pair over the coming sessions ahead now.
