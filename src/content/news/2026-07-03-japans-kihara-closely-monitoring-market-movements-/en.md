Japan’s Chief Cabinet Secretary Minoru Kihara reiterated on Friday during the European session that authorities are closely monitoring foreign exchange (FX) market movements with a high sense of urgency and remain alert to excessive volatility. He emphasized that the administration is prepared to respond swiftly if needed, signaling continued vigilance toward yen fluctuations and broader currency stability in global markets.

On Tuesday, Kihara also stated that officials are always ready to take necessary action in the foreign exchange market, reinforcing Japan’s stance on intervention readiness. The comments come amid ongoing scrutiny of yen volatility, as policymakers continue to watch speculative moves closely and signal willingness to step in should disorderly or rapid currency shifts occur again in markets if necessary.
