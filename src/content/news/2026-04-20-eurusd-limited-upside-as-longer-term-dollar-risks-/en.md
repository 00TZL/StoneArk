EUR/USD is showing limited upside in the near term, according to Commerzbank’s Thu Lan Nguyen. The analyst suggests that recent gains could be constrained as markets may be overestimating the European Central Bank’s response to the latest inflation data. While the euro has seen some support from optimism over the ECB’s stance, Nguyen notes that the currency pair faces headwinds as investor expectations could be ahead of the central bank’s actual moves.  

Longer-term risks for the dollar remain elevated, keeping EUR/USD gains in check. Nguyen highlights that while short-term movements may appear positive for the euro, structural factors favoring the dollar—including safe-haven demand and US economic resilience—could limit the currency pair’s potential upside over coming weeks.
