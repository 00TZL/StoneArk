The Indian Rupee (INR) strengthened sharply against the US Dollar (USD) on Friday after Indian markets reopened following the Bakri Id holiday. The USD/INR pair slipped toward 95.40 as declining crude oil prices boosted sentiment around the Indian currency.

Oil prices fell after renewed optimism surrounding a potential United States (US)-Iran deal raised expectations of improved global oil supply. Since India is one of the world’s largest oil importers, lower crude prices typically support the Rupee by easing inflation and reducing the country’s import bill.
