The Canadian Dollar (CAD) is gaining strength as market sentiment improves following the announcement of a US-Iran ceasefire. USD/CAD continues to lose ground for the third consecutive day, trading around 1.3830 during the Asian session on Wednesday. The pair's decline reflects a weaker US Dollar, which is under pressure due to reduced demand for safe-haven assets.

The decline in the USD comes as investors grow more optimistic after the United States and Iran agreed to a two-week ceasefire, easing geopolitical tensions. With risk appetite improving, the Canadian Dollar is benefiting from the shift in sentiment, pushing USD/CAD lower in the early part of the trading day.
