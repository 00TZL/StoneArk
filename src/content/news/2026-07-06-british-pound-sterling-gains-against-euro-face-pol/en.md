Sterling extended its gains against the Euro, with ING saying the recent downside break in EUR/GBP has been driven by the unwinding of stale short Sterling positions. The bank added that lower foreign exchange volatility has also reduced investors’ willingness to pay carry, providing additional support for the British currency.

However, ING cautioned that Sterling’s rally could soon face a political test. While technical and positioning factors have underpinned the Pound’s advance, any renewed political uncertainty in the UK could challenge the currency’s momentum. For now, the combination of short-covering and subdued FX volatility remains the key driver behind Sterling’s strength versus the Euro.
