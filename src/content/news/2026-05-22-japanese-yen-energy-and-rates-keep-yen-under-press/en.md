The Japanese Yen (JPY) continues to face downward pressure, according to Commerzbank’s Volkmar Baur. Despite April’s inflation showing only a slight softening, the currency has struggled as investors remain wary of Japan’s persistent high debt levels. Global interest rate hikes are adding to the strain, making the Yen less attractive compared to higher-yielding alternatives. Baur notes that these factors have kept the Yen on the back foot, even as domestic inflation figures suggest only moderate economic shifts.

The broader environment of rising borrowing costs worldwide and Japan’s fiscal challenges has prevented any sustained recovery in the Yen. Market participants are watching for policy signals and inflation trends, but for now, the currency is expected to remain under pressure. Analysts highlight that until there are clear signs of either easing debt concerns or a more competitive interest rate environment, the Yen is likely to trade weaker against major peers.
