The Japanese Yen (JPY) continues to show signs of weakness, even during softer sessions for the US Dollar (USD), according to ING's Francesco Pesole. Traders appear to be testing the limits of official tolerance, probing how far the Yen can slide before intervention measures are taken. Despite occasional dips in the USD, the JPY has not demonstrated meaningful recovery, highlighting a market that is increasingly willing to challenge the Bank of Japan’s defensive stance.

Pesole suggests that the credibility of intervention is fading as the Yen remains vulnerable against the greenback. The persistence of this weakness signals that market participants are more focused on risk-adjusted opportunities than on potential policy action. As a result, the Yen’s response to USD fluctuations remains muted, leaving investors to navigate a landscape where official support may no longer guarantee stability.
