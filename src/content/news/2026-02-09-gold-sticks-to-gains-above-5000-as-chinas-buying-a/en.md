Gold (XAU/USD) held its ground above the key $5,000 level during the Asian session on Monday, bolstered by recent developments surrounding China's gold buying and expectations of a U.S. Federal Reserve rate cut. The surge comes after the People's Bank of China (PBOC) extended its gold purchases for the 15th consecutive month in January, fueling increased demand for the precious metal.

This extended buying streak, combined with growing market speculation that the Fed may cut rates in the near future, has further boosted gold's appeal as a safe-haven asset. With geopolitical tensions and inflation concerns lingering, investors continue to flock to gold, driving its prices higher amid a supportive macroeconomic backdrop.
