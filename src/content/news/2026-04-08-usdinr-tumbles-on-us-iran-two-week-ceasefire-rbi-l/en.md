The Indian Rupee (INR) surged to a near three-week high against the US Dollar (USD) in early trade on Wednesday, with the USD/INR pair dropping to around 92.30. This move followed a weakening of the US Dollar and a sharp decline in global oil prices, after the US and Iran agreed to a two-week ceasefire. 

In domestic developments, the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) kept its key interest rate unchanged at 5.25%, maintaining its cautious stance amidst ongoing inflation concerns. The combination of favorable geopolitical news and a steady RBI policy helped the INR strengthen, marking a positive start to the day for the Indian currency.
