The US Dollar Index (DXY), which measures the Greenback's performance against a basket of currencies, continues to struggle, hovering near a four-year low around 96.00. This decline comes amid ongoing concerns regarding the Federal Reserve's policy decisions and mounting economic risks, leading to increased selling pressure during the Asian session on Thursday. 

As a result, the DXY has surrendered some of the modest gains it achieved in the previous trading day. Market participants remain cautious, weighing the implications of potential Fed actions and broader economic indicators, which could further impact the dollar's trajectory in the near term.
