Australian Dollar extended gains on Monday, supported by improved risk sentiment after the United States and Iran announced a framework agreement aimed at ending their conflict. The move lifted demand for risk-sensitive currencies, with traders rotating out of safe-haven assets ahead of key central bank events.

AUD/USD trades around 0.7070 at the time of writing, up 0.35% on the day, as the Australian dollar benefits from the broader risk-on tone. Market participants are positioning ahead of the Reserve Bank of Australia decision, which is expected to guide near-term direction. The pair remains sensitive to global risk flows and US monetary policy expectations. Volatility is likely to persist as traders assess inflation signals and rate outlook in coming sessions overall bias.
