Silver dipped to a three-week low on Monday, with XAG/USD slipping 0.5% to hover near $80.00 in late Asian trading. The metal traded as low as $78.00 during the session, marking its weakest level since early March. Traders appear cautious ahead of the Federal Reserve’s policy announcement on Wednesday, as market consensus leans toward a hold in interest rates.  

Market participants are closely watching the Fed for clues on the future direction of U.S. monetary policy, with expectations of a pause fueling subdued momentum in silver. The recent pullback reflects both profit-taking and lingering uncertainty, leaving XAG/USD testing key support levels around $78.00 as investors weigh potential reactions to the upcoming central bank statement.
