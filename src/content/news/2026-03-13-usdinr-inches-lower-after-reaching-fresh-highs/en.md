The USD/INR pair saw a modest decline on Friday, following its recent surge to an all-time high of 92.90 in the previous session. The rupee's weakness persisted, as investors reacted to the continued pressure on the currency from global market conditions and domestic factors.

Despite hitting new highs, the Indian rupee faced downward pressure in early trading on Friday, reflecting broader market sentiment and the strength of the U.S. dollar. Market participants remain cautious, watching for further economic data and geopolitical developments that may impact the pair in the coming sessions. The pair's short-term outlook depends on how both domestic and global factors evolve, with a focus on U.S. economic indicators and inflation data.
