The Japanese yen traded largely flat against the US dollar on Friday, as growing concerns over possible Japanese government intervention helped counter weaker-than-expected Tokyo CPI data and expectations surrounding future Federal Reserve policy moves. The mixed fundamental backdrop kept investors cautious, limiting directional momentum in the currency market.

Meanwhile, USD/JPY struggled to build on a modest intraday rebound, although the pair remained supported above the 159.00 level during the early European session. Traders continued to balance softer inflation signals from Japan against intervention risks and evolving Fed rate expectations, leaving the pair confined to a narrow range despite underlying bullish pressure on the dollar.
