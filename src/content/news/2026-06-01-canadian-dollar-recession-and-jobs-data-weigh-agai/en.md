The Canadian Dollar weakened against the US Dollar, with sentiment pressured by softer oil prices and confirmation that Canada slipped into an unexpected technical recession, according to Brown Brothers Harriman (BBH) strategist Elias Haddad. The combination of weaker commodity support and deteriorating economic conditions reduced demand for the loonie, leaving it among the weaker major currencies during the session.

Additional pressure came from disappointing Canadian labor market data, which reinforced concerns about slowing domestic growth and increased expectations that policymakers may need to maintain a more accommodative stance. In contrast, the US Dollar remained supported by its relative resilience, allowing USD/CAD to stay firm as investors weighed Canada's economic challenges against a still-uncertain global outlook.
