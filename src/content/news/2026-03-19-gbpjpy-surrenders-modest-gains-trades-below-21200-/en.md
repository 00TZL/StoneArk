The GBP/JPY pair gave up earlier gains on Thursday, sliding below the 212.00 mark after touching 212.35 during the Asian session. Traders appear cautious as the cross fails to sustain momentum, retreating to the lower end of its daily range in the final hour of trading. Market participants are eyeing key developments from both the Bank of Japan and the Bank of England, which are set to influence the yen and sterling outlooks.  

Investor sentiment remains tentative ahead of the BoJ policy announcement and the BoE interest rate decision. With modest upside from earlier Asian session highs fading, GBP/JPY is now consolidating below 212.00, reflecting uncertainty in the market and the potential for volatility around these central bank events.
