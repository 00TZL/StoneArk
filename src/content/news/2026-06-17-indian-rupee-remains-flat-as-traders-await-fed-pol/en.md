Indian Rupee traded largely flat against the US Dollar on Wednesday, pausing after two consecutive sessions of gains. The currency struggled to find fresh momentum as traders remained cautious ahead of the Federal Reserve policy decision, keeping overall volatility subdued in the USD/INR pair.

Meanwhile, the upside in USD/INR appears capped in the near term as downward pressure on the rupee continues to ease. Support for the Indian currency is emerging from softer global crude oil prices, which reduce import bill concerns and improve sentiment. However, market participants remain focused on the Fed outcome, which is expected to guide near-term direction for emerging market currencies including the rupee. Until then, range-bound trading is likely amid cautious positioning in the market.
