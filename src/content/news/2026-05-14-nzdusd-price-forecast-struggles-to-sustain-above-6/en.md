The NZD/USD pair is facing mild downside pressure as it hovers around 0.5935 during Thursday’s late Asian session. Traders appear hesitant to push the Kiwi above the key 61.8% Fibonacci retracement level near 0.5940, indicating that the upward momentum is struggling to sustain itself. Market participants are watching closely for any signs of a breakout, but for now, the pair remains constrained below this technical resistance.

Support levels near 0.5920 and 0.5910 could provide short-term floors if selling pressure intensifies, while a firm break above 0.5940 may open the door for further gains toward 0.5960. The market’s cautious tone suggests that the NZD/USD could continue to consolidate in the near term, as traders weigh economic data and risk sentiment across global markets.
