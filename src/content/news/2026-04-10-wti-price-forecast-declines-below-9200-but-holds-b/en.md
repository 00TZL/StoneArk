WTI crude oil is trading at $91.75 during early European hours on Friday, marking a slight decline from earlier levels. The pullback comes as market participants prepare for the high-stakes talks between the US and Iran, set to take place in Pakistan on Saturday. These negotiations are seen as crucial for determining the future direction of oil supply and geopolitical tensions in the region.

Despite the recent dip, WTI holds a bullish bias, supported by ongoing concerns over global oil supply and potential disruptions. Traders will closely monitor the talks, as any breakthrough or setback could significantly impact prices in the near term, with $92.00 serving as a key support level to watch.
