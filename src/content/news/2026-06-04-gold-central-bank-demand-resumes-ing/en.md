Central banks resumed net purchases of gold in April, reversing March’s net sales, according to data highlighted by ING. The shift indicates renewed appetite for the safe-haven asset, reflecting ongoing strategies to diversify reserves amid global economic uncertainty. Analysts note that these central bank moves often signal confidence in gold’s long-term value, even as market volatility persists.

The World Gold Council’s data underlines this rebound, showing that institutional buyers collectively increased holdings after a brief pause. ING emphasizes that such demand from central banks can influence broader market sentiment, supporting gold prices and potentially affecting currency and bond markets. This renewed buying pattern underscores gold’s enduring role as a strategic reserve asset.
