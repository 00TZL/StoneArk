China’s Commerce Ministry said during Thursday’s European session that it firmly opposes all forms of unilateral tariffs imposed by the United States, responding to Washington’s proposal to introduce new tariffs linked to alleged forced labor concerns. The ministry reiterated Beijing’s long-standing position that trade restrictions should not be used as a political tool and urged the US to adhere to international trade rules.

The comments come as investors continue to monitor escalating trade tensions between the world’s two largest economies. Chinese officials warned that additional tariff measures could disrupt global supply chains and undermine normal trade relations. Market participants are closely watching developments for potential implications on risk sentiment, commodity demand, and major currency pairs sensitive to shifts in global trade dynamics.
