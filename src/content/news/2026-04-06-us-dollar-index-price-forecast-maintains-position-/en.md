The US Dollar Index (DXY) held steady above the 100.00 level on Monday, trading near 100.10 during Asian sessions, after climbing for two consecutive days. The index, which tracks the US Dollar (USD) against a basket of six major currencies, continues to find support around its nine-day exponential moving average (EMA), signaling resilience despite slight intraday pullbacks.  

Traders are watching the DXY closely as it consolidates in this range, with the USD showing mixed performance against its major counterparts. Market participants are weighing economic data and central bank signals, which could influence the index’s next move. For now, the dollar remains broadly supported, keeping the index above the psychologically significant 100.00 mark.
