Silver (XAG/USD) resumed its downtrend on Tuesday after a brief, tame recovery attempt on Monday, with prices sliding back toward the mid-range of $62.00. The metal is edging closer to seven-month lows around the $61.00 area, signaling renewed bearish pressure in the market as sellers regain control following the short-lived rebound.

Market sentiment remains pressured by a sharp rise in US yields, which continues to weigh on non-yielding assets like silver. Traders are closely monitoring macroeconomic signals for direction, as higher real yields reduce demand for precious metals. Despite intermittent stabilization attempts, XAG/USD remains vulnerable to further downside unless bond market dynamics ease or risk appetite improves across broader financial markets in the near-term outlook for precious metals overall tone.
