Gold (XAU/USD) rebounded on Friday, clawing back some of the losses seen in the previous session. The yellow metal gained ground as investor sentiment shifted toward safe-haven assets, lifting the broader precious metals market. Traders cited renewed demand for hedges amid lingering economic uncertainties, which helped support gold’s recovery. Despite the uptick, the pace of buying remained measured, with investors cautious ahead of upcoming economic data that could influence market direction.  

Even with Friday’s gains, gold is still poised for a weekly decline as pressure from a stronger dollar and higher U.S. Treasury yields weighed on prices earlier in the week. Analysts note that while short-term rebounds offer relief, broader market trends continue to favor downward momentum for the week.
