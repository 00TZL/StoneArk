Gold (XAU/USD) fell to a two-week low, hovering around the $4,758-$4,757 mark during the Asian session on Friday. This decline marks the first weekly loss for the precious metal in five weeks, as bearish sentiment continues to dominate the market.

Concerns surrounding geopolitical tensions in Iran and persistent inflation fears have bolstered the U.S. dollar, further pressuring gold prices. As the dollar strengthens, investors are increasingly cautious, leading to a sell-off in gold and a shift in focus towards safer assets amid economic uncertainties. Market participants will be watching closely for any developments that could influence these trends in the coming sessions.
