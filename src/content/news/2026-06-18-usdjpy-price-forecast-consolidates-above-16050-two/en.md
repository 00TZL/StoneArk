USD/JPY extends its sideways consolidation into the early European session on Thursday, holding just above the 160.50 area and trading in the mid-160s. The pair remains pinned near its highest level since July 2024, after touching that zone in the previous session. Price action reflects limited directional momentum as traders weigh persistent yen weakness.

Market participants remain cautious as the pair’s sustained strength keeps intervention risks in focus, with Japanese authorities previously signaling discomfort around rapid yen depreciation. However, lack of fresh catalysts has allowed USD/JPY to consolidate rather than extend higher in a straight line, as investors assess policy divergence between the Federal Reserve and the Bank of Japan near-term outlook remains range-bound amid volatility and liquidity concerns easing.
