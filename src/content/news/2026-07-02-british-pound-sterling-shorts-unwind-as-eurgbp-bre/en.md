Sterling came under pressure as EUR/GBP broke below the 0.8600/0.8610 support zone, according to ING. The move triggered a sharp adjustment in positioning, with previously extended Sterling shorts beginning to unwind as price action accelerated through key technical levels during early trading.

ING strategist Chris Turner notes that EUR/GBP's break through the 0.8600/0.8610 support has encouraged liquidation of expensive, stale Sterling short positions, particularly among asset managers who were positioned for continued downside in the pair. Flows are expected to remain volatile as technical breakouts force further covering, while traders reassess near-term support levels and positioning dynamics in the currency pair with market focus shifting toward ECB-BOE rate expectations and broader risk sentiment across European FX into late London trading.
