The Indian Rupee (INR) edged higher against the US Dollar (USD) during Wednesday’s opening trade, supported by softer crude oil prices and improved market sentiment. Lower oil prices offered relief to the Indian currency, given India’s heavy reliance on crude imports, while traders also monitored foreign fund flows and regional market cues.

However, gains in the Rupee remained limited as uncertainty surrounding Iran and broader Middle East tensions continued to keep investors cautious. The US Dollar held relatively firm amid safe-haven demand, with market participants awaiting further geopolitical developments and upcoming economic data for fresh direction in the USD/INR pair.
